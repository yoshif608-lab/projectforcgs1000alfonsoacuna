Future Career

Combining a cybersecurity foundation with a data analysis degree opens several career paths. Here are three roles I'm researching:

Data Analyst

Companies: Firms like Deloitte, JPMorgan Chase, and Citigroup regularly hire graduates from analytics programs for this role.

What I'd do: Collect, clean, and interpret datasets, then build reports and dashboards that help teams make decisions.

Business Intelligence (BI) Analyst

Companies: Mid-to-large companies across finance, retail, and healthcare (e.g., regional firms in the Tampa/Orlando corridor).

What I'd do: Design dashboards and BI tools, track KPIs, and translate business questions into data queries.

Security-Focused Data Analyst

Companies: Cybersecurity vendors and government-adjacent agencies that value both data and security skills.

What I'd do: Analyze security and network data to spot anomalies and support risk decisions — directly using my HCC cybersecurity background.

Related occupations tracked by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics — such as operations research analysts — show strong momentum: employment in that field is projected to grow 21% from 2024–2034, much faster than average, with a median annual wage of about $91,290.
