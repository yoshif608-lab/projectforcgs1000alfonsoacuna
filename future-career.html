Future Career

Combining a cybersecurity foundation with a nice data analysis degree opens various career paths. Here are three roles I'm looking for:

Data Analyst

Companies: Firms like Netdata, JPMorgan Chase, and Cisco regularly hire graduates from analytics programs for this role.

What I'd do: Collect, clean, and interpret datasets, then make reports and dashboards that help teams make decisions.

Business Intelligence (BI) Analyst

Companies: I would like companies across finance, retail, and healthcare.

What I'd do: Design dashboards and BI tools, track KPIs, and translate business questions into data queries, maybe even more if it is required.

Security-Focused Data Analyst

Companies: Cybersecurity vendors and government-adjacent agencies that value both data and security skills.

What I'd do: Analyze security and network data to spot anomalies and support risk decisions, using my background learned in HC, Netdata and some certifications like PaloAlto's one.

Related occupations tracked by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, such as operations research analysts, show decent momentum: employment in that field is expected to grow a 21% from 2024–2034, much faster than average, with a median annual wage of about $91,290, which is decent.
